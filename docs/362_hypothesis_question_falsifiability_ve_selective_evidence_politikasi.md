# Phase 71: Hypothesis, Question Falsifiability & Selective Evidence Policy

## Falsifiability
Every question and hypothesis must be framed such that it can be proven false. "Vague narrative-only" prompts are rejected by the canonical registry.

## Question Framing
Questions must have clear independent and dependent variables. They must reside within a defined scope (e.g., specific symbols, time regimes).

## Evidence Balance
Evidence bundles cannot be one-sided. Supportive and contradictory evidence must be explicitly separated and weighed.

## Contradiction Coverage
A hypothesis must acknowledge contradictory findings. Ignoring contrary data triggers validation failures and degrades the research trust verdict.

## The Risks of Selective Evidence
Selecting only favorable evidence introduces confirmation bias, directly polluting strategy promotion. The system mandates tracking of weak or contradictory evidence to mitigate this risk.
