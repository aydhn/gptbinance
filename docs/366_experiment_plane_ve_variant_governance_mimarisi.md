# Experiment Plane and Variant Governance Architecture

## Objective -> Arms -> Fairness -> Stopping -> Recommendation

This plane establishes the strict comparison rules and governance needed to confidently draw conclusions from experiments.

## Why Baseline/Control Discipline Matters
We cannot just launch a strategy variant, see its return, and call it a winner. We need a specific baseline or control group running concurrently, under the same data truth and regime.

## Why Comparable != Just Same Time Period
Execution frictions, risk block triggers, partial fills, and slippage can vary significantly. An arm with relaxed risk limits might outperform but cannot be compared directly with an arm with strict risk limits.

## Governance
No single arm metric is an answer. A trust verdict must be generated that asserts the fairness, bias absence, baseline integrity, and equivalence of the experiment before promotion recommendations are produced.
