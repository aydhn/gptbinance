# Responsibility, Culpability, and Fault Policies

## Distinction of Core Concepts
- **Responsibility:** Structural accountability for a domain or process.
- **Culpability:** The moral or legal blameworthiness (e.g., intentional vs. negligent).
- **Fault:** The specific failure in duties or execution.
- **Negligence:** Failure to exercise required care.
- **Strict Liability:** Liability regardless of fault.
- **Contribution:** Material impact leading to harm.
- **Indemnity:** Contractual shifting of financial burden.
- **Exoneration:** Validated removal of liability.

## Why Compensation != Exoneration
Providing compensation covers financial or operational damages but does not automatically extinguish historical liability, nor does it guarantee the removal of residual exposure.
