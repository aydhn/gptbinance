# Estate, Claim, Priority, Stay, Moratorium, and Avoidance Policy

## Estate Management
Estates are formed dynamically around insolvency triggers. An estate explicitly categorizes all included, excluded, and disputed assets.

## Claims and Priority
- **Secured**: Must have explicitly validated collateral; false secured comfort is flagged.
- **Priority**: Must rely on statutory or beneficiary-protective laws, strictly tracked to avoid priority inflation.
- **Unsecured**: General claims and residual deficiencies.
- **Subordination**: Structural or contractual demotions of claims must be canonical.

## Stays and Moratoria
Stays are not universal freezes; they are scoped. Attempting enforcement against a covered scope produces an explicit anomaly.

## Avoidance
Transfers out of the estate window must be analyzed for preference and fraud-like qualities. Avoidance claims must map directly to expected estate recapture.
